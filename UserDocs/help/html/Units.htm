Units of Measurement

There are commands to enter measurement units into your equations. Type for example \kg, \lbm, \m, \s, \kW, \day... Commands for measurement units are case sensitive!

Special cases: \deg (° - degree), \’ (minute of arc), \’’ (second of arc), \degC (°C), \ohm (resistance), \mn (minute)

Shortcuts for some compound units are also available: \mps (m/s), \radps (rad/s), \Apm (A/m), \kgpm (kg/m), \Vpm (V/m), \Nm (Nm), \Ns (Ns)

How to enter: kg/m3? Write the following command \kgpm, and then press ALT+3.

Measurement units are displayed in a bit pale color.
